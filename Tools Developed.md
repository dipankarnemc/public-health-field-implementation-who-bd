# Tools Developed  
WHO Bangladesh – NCD & Mental Health Programme  
Author: Doctor Dipankar Das  

This document lists the tools I developed or contributed to during my work with the WHO Bangladesh NCD and Mental Health programme. All tools are shared in a non-confidential format and reflect only general structures and learning resources.

---

## 1. Mental Health Sentinel Surveillance Data Collection Tool

### Purpose
To support the pilot implementation of mental health sentinel surveillance in Chapainawabganj, Sylhet, and Jashore.

### Key Features
- Structured form for capturing mental health presentations at the PHC level  
- Sections for demographic details, presenting complaints, diagnosis, and referrals  
- Built-in guidance on data confidentiality and storage  
- Standardized coding for priority conditions  

### My Contribution
- Developed the full tool structure  
- Trained UHC staff on data collection and data protection in Manirampur UHC  
- Provided ongoing feedback during early data collection rounds  

---

## 2. mhGAP Data Reporting Template

### Purpose
To help PHC providers document and report cases using the mhGAP Essential Care module.

### Key Features
- Case summary sheet (daily/weekly)  
- Referral and follow-up tracking fields  
- Monthly compilation sheet consistent with DGHS reporting formats  

### My Contribution
- Created a simplified reporting template to support new providers  
- Facilitated hands-on training on mhGAP data reporting during two district trainings  
- Provided corrections and follow-up support based on early reporting errors  

---

## 3. Suicide Surveillance Volunteer Reporting Sheet

### Purpose
To support community-level identification and reporting of individuals at risk of suicide.

### Key Features
- Simple checklist-based form for volunteers  
- Fields covering warning signs, initial actions taken, and referrals  
- Clear guidance on when and how to escalate urgent cases  

### My Contribution
- Contributed to adapting the tool with BCCP based on field needs  
- Guided volunteers during community sessions in Jashore and Jhenaidah  

---

## 4. Tele-Mental Health Centre Client Log Sheet

### Purpose
To standardize case documentation within tele-mental health centres in Jashore’s UHCs.

### Key Features
- Client registration fields  
- Presenting issues and referral category  
- Follow-up tracking  
- Data validation prompts for monthly reporting  

### My Contribution
- Provided technical input during development  
- Supported centre staff during on-site orientations  
- Ensured alignment with mental health indicators used by DGHS  

---

## 5. School and Community Awareness Session Outline

### Purpose
To support the structured delivery of mental health and suicide awareness sessions.

### Key Features
- Session plan (45–60 minutes)  
- Key messages tailored for adolescents and community members  
- Activity list (story-based discussion, Q&A, help-seeking map)  

### My Contribution
- Prepared outline used across three schools and one community meeting  
- Delivered awareness sessions and facilitated interactive activities  

---

## 6. Healthy Workplace Quick Guide

### Purpose
To promote the adoption of WHO Healthy Workplace practices in government settings.

### Key Features
- Simple checklist for workplace wellness actions  
- Recommendations on hydration, physical activity, and tobacco-free environments  
- Tips for activity breaks during long meetings and trainings  

### My Contribution
- Developed and shared the guide with CSOs and UHC leaders  
- Encouraged actions like replacing plastic water bottles and choosing healthy snacks  

---

## 7. NCD & Mental Health Field Monitoring Checklist

### Purpose
To support field assessments in UHCs and tele-mental health centres.

### Key Features
- Service availability & readiness indicators  
- Provider competency checklist  
- Data reporting and documentation review section  
- Area for action points and follow-up timeline  

### My Contribution
- Created the monitoring structure  
- Used the checklist during field supervision visits with Moner Bondhu  
- Updated content based on field observations
