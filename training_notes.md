# Training Notes  
WHO Bangladesh – NCD & Mental Health Programme  
Author: Doctor Dipankar Das  

This document summarizes key training sessions I contributed to during my tenure with the WHO Bangladesh NCD and Mental Health programme. The notes cover objectives, methods, tools used, and reflections from district-level capacity-building activities in Jashore and Jhenaidah.

---

## 1. mhGAP Training (Jashore & Jhenaidah)

### Objectives
- Build the capacity of PHC service providers to identify and manage priority mental health conditions.
- Strengthen the integration of mental health services at the UHC level.

### My Contributions
- Facilitated sessions on:
  - **mhGAP Essential Care**
  - **Data reporting using the mhGAP monitoring system**
- Supported selection of participants and coordination with CSOs and UHFPOs.
- Provided follow-up support to trained providers through supportive supervision.

### Key Takeaways
- Providers showed improved understanding of depression, suicide, and substance use management.
- More support is required to improve documentation and follow-up care.

---

## 2. Suicide Surveillance Training (BCCP)

### Objectives
- Equip Field Assistants, Field Research Supervisors, and volunteers to identify, report, and refer individuals at suicide risk.
- Strengthen community-level surveillance and awareness activities.

### My Contributions
- Provided technical input during planning and delivery of training modules.
- Participated in training sessions in:
  - **Chougacha, Jashore (August 2024)**
  - **Shoilkupa, Jhenaidah (October 28–29, 2024)**
  - **Chougacha, Jashore (October 30–31, 2024)**
- Conducted community and school-level awareness discussions.

### Key Takeaways
- Volunteers gained confidence in identifying warning signs and guiding people to services.
- Community engagement improved understanding of mental health and reduced stigma.

---

## 3. Tele-Mental Health Centre Orientation

### Objectives
- Introduce UHC teams to the tele-mental health model.
- Improve clinical and data reporting practices.

### My Contributions
- Supported orientation and on-site demonstration in six UHCs of Jashore.
- Guided case logging and referral pathways.

---

## 4. Sentinel Surveillance – Mental Health

### Objectives
- Pilot structured mental health surveillance in selected districts.

### My Contributions
- Developed a data collection tool used in **Chapainawabganj, Sylhet, and Jashore**.
- Facilitated training on data collection and data protection at **Manirampur UHC**.

### Key Takeaways
- Staff gained clarity on confidentiality, data accuracy, and reporting responsibilities.

---

## 5. Health Promotion Trainings & Events

### Activities Supported
- World No Tobacco Day
- World Hypertension Day
- Mental Health awareness sessions
- Healthy Workplace promotion with government officials

### My Contributions
- Delivered sessions on:
  - Physical activity and NCD prevention
  - Healthy lifestyle practices
  - Workplace wellness actions (e.g., reducing plastic bottle use, healthy snacks, short activity breaks)

---

## Overall Reflections
- Training at the district and UHC levels significantly strengthened provider confidence.
- Continued supervision and refresher training are essential to maintain quality.
- Community engagement combined with clinical training creates a stronger support system for mental health and NCD care.
